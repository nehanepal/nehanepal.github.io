Wind Turbine Performance Modeling

Blade Element Momentum Theory + Wake Analysis

Modeled the aerodynamic performance of a horizontal-axis wind turbine using MATLAB and Blade Element Momentum theory. The turbine used an S8037 airfoil, four blades, a 50-meter rotor diameter, and a tip speed ratio of 6 at a rated wind speed of 12 m/s.

MATLAB BEM Theory Aerodynamics SolidWorks Wake Modeling
Rotor 50 m diameter
Rated Speed 12 m/s
Output 1.03 MW predicted power

Technical Work

  • Estimated Reynolds number, chord, pitch, and axial induction factor distributions.
  • Calculated power, torque, and thrust coefficients through iterative MATLAB modeling.
  • Created and refined a 3D blade model in SolidWorks.
  • Analyzed wake behavior using Park, Revised Park, and Entrainment models.

Results

  • Predicted 1.03 MW of turbine power generation.
  • Evaluated root bending moment and critical deflection wind speed.
  • Modeled 10-turbine farm behavior using wake interaction analysis.
  • Strengthened aerodynamic modeling and simulation workflow skills.
